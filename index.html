The Four Main Types of Coffee Beans

Coffee lovers around the world enjoy a diverse range of flavors, aromas, and textures, all originating from four primary coffee bean varieties.
Understanding these beans is essential for selecting the right type for your business or personal enjoyment.
Below is a detailed guide to the four main types of coffee beans: Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, and Excelsa.

Main Types of Coffee Beans

Arabica (Coffea arabica)

Arabica coffee makes up 60–70% of global production and is prized for its smooth, complex flavor and lower caffeine content.

Flavor Profile:

  • Sweet, soft taste with hints of fruit, nuts, chocolate, and floral notes.
  • Often has a light acidity and smooth finish.
  • Some high-altitude Arabica beans develop a wine-like quality.

Growing Conditions:

  • Requires high altitudes (600–2,000 meters above sea level).
  • Prefers cooler climates with consistent rainfall.
  • Grown in Latin America, Ethiopia, Kenya, Yemen, and other regions.

Caffeine Content:

  • Lower than other varieties (~1.2% caffeine).

Uses:

  • Premium, specialty, and high-end coffee brands.
  • Often used in single-origin coffees and gourmet blends.

Robusta (Coffea canephora)

Robusta is the second most popular coffee bean, accounting for 30–40% of global production. It is known for its strong, bold flavor and higher caffeine content.

Flavor Profile:

  • Earthy, nutty, and bitter with a heavier body.
  • Less acidity than Arabica.
  • Often has chocolate or grainy undertones.

Growing Conditions:

  • Grows at lower altitudes (200–800 meters).
  • Thrives in warmer, humid climates.
  • Major producers include Vietnam, Brazil, Indonesia, and parts of Africa.

Caffeine Content:

  • Almost double that of Arabica (~2.2% caffeine).

Uses:

  • Commonly found in instant coffee, espresso blends, and budget-friendly brands.
  • Adds crema and strength to espresso blends.

Liberica (Coffea liberica)

Liberica is a rare and unique coffee variety that represents less than 2% of global production. It is mainly grown in Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

Flavor Profile:

  • Distinctive smoky, woody, and floral flavors.
  • Sometimes has a fruity or nutty aftertaste.
  • Bold and full-bodied with aromatic complexity.

Growing Conditions:

  • Grows at low to medium altitudes.
  • Thrives in hot and humid climates.
  • Mainly produced in the Philippines, Malaysia, and West Africa.

Caffeine Content:

  • Moderate (~1.2% caffeine).

Uses:

  • Often enjoyed by those looking for a unique and exotic coffee experience.
  • Common in regional specialty coffees.

Excelsa (Coffea excelsa)

Excelsa is a subvariety of Liberica with distinct characteristics. It accounts for less than 1% of global coffee production and is mainly grown in Southeast Asia.

Flavor Profile:

  • Complex mix of tart, fruity, and slightly spiced flavors.
  • Often has a light body with a lingering aftertaste.
  • Balances between sweet and sharp notes.

Growing Conditions:

  • Grows in medium-altitude regions.
  • Thrives in warm, tropical climates.
  • Mainly produced in the Philippines, Vietnam, and Southeast Asia.

Caffeine Content:

  • Medium (~1.0–1.3% caffeine).

Uses:

  • Commonly blended with other beans to enhance complexity.
  • Adds depth and variety to specialty blends.

Which Coffee Bean Should You Choose?

Each coffee bean type has its own strengths. Your choice depends on taste and goals:

  • Premium, high-quality: Go for Arabica.
  • Strong and affordable: Choose Robusta.
  • Exotic and rare: Try Liberica or Excelsa.
